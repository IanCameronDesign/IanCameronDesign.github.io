About

    When I was younger I used to love to draw, my mother encouraged me to apply to the Visual Arts Program at Mayfield Secondary School. I took classes in video editing, photography, and even learning autocad in architecture class as I explored my creative interests.

    Exploring

    After High School I took a Design Certificate Course where I explored multiple design disciplines like Graphic, and Packaging before deciding I want to become an Industrial Designer and create products people can hold in their hands.

  • 2013

    My First Gig

    I completed my Industrial Design Degree and got my first job at a Sky2Designs where I worked on new product ideas and existing Solar powered products. I gained valuable knowledge of manufacturing techniques for a variety of different materials.

  • 2015

    Gaining Experience

    I worked as the Production and Design Manager at 3D Phacktory. Here I worked on a wide range of products either managing projects or working with clients myself to design and produce everything from Movie props to medical devices to childrens toys. I also got to run the most advanced 3D printer in the city.

Xogo

Mobile Gaming Controller

As technology advances in the mobile phone market demand for more immersive games will increase. The problem facing gamers today is that a touchscreen interaction with a game is not a viable solution for an intensive gaming experience. Touchscreens are not able to give tactile physical feed back as to the location of hands or actions because they only provide a single smooth plane as the point of interaction. Touchscreen controls allow you to interact with simple relatively 2D (in the interaction) games, however they are unable to provide interaction within a virtual environment through a character like for example in a first person shooter. My goal is to provide consumers with a portable, tactile interface that will allow gamers a more in depth experience with their portable games.

  • Date: 2013
  • Client: Thesis Project
  • Category: Consumer Electronics

The Vault

Home cloud computing and storage device

The Vault utilizes the new technology of Lifi. Lifi is a technology created by Harold Haas who uses LED lights to send data and information over lightwaves rather than radiowaves. In this project we were asked to design a product that used the features and benefits of a new technology. Because lightwaves cannot penetrate through walls like radiowaves this makes a Lifi network many times more secure than a traditional wifi network. Using this benfit the Vault stores all your personal information and media in one central hub in the house which is then dispersed through the lighting in the house to TVs, tablets, and phones. Adding swappable harddrive keeps your library of goods mobile for students and professionals.

  • Year: 2012
  • Client: School Project
  • Category: Advanced Technology

Octo

Professional Massage Device.

As the sole designer at Thumper Massager I was tasked with redesigning their flagship product to be battery powered. The existing product used a power cord and the company wanted to invest in updating the product line to battery powered for more mobility. Utilizing the mechanical parts I received from the engineer I was able to design both a new 12V Battery, a charging nest and the new massager device. Working closely with the owner and the assembly plant manager we created a new product that was easier to assemble and reduced the cost compared to the previous model. Working with both local and foreign manufacturers I was able to increase efficency of production.

  • Date: 2016-2017
  • Client: Thumper Massager
  • Category: Redesign - Consumer Electronics

Jawz

Apline Safety Mask and Communicator

Jawz is a multi-functional Alpine face mask with wireless communictations built right in. In this group project we learned about Bayer Material Science’s different plastic materials. We applied a variety of recyclable Bayer materials to this product to minimize environmental impact from production. The Jawz system not only protects riders’ faces form the elements it also connects riders across the slopes to keep everyone safe and together in large ski resorts and outback conditions.

  • Date: 2013
  • Client: Bayer - School Project
  • Category: Athletic Gear
